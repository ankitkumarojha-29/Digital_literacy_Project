Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students

Do's:
1. Do use privacy settings to strictly control who can see your personal posts and information.
2. Do keep your LinkedIn and GitHub profiles updated with your latest academic projects and coding skills.
3. Do think critically before sharing articles or forwarded messages to avoid spreading misinformation.
4. Do use professional language when interacting with professors, university accounts, or companies online.
5. Do assume that everything you post on the internet can eventually be seen by a future employer.

Don'ts:
1. Don't post negative, emotional, or derogatory comments about your university, peers, or faculty members.
2. Don't share sensitive personal details like your exact location, phone number, or banking information.
3. Don't engage in public arguments, flame wars, or cyberbullying in comment sections.
4. Don't post photos containing illegal activities or extreme unprofessional behavior.
5. Don't log into your personal social media accounts on public college lab computers without securely logging out afterward.
